How Does One Become Not Susceptible to Fact Manipulation?

Introduction

The main goal of teaching voters to tell fact from fiction in this presidential election is composed of the following TWO sub-targets: 1) develop the habit of using fact-checking websites, 2) have empathy for other people who could be socially, financially and culturally different from the voters themselves in order to understand contexts behind people’s decision-making process.

That is, in order to achieve the goal of teaching voters to tell fact from fiction in the next presidential election, each sub-target is a requisite piece of the larger objective. The learning target will be achieved if/when learners can identify misstatements by the candidates. In order to successfully teach this topic, let’s revisit the following THREE major content pieces.
